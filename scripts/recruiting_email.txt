Subject: In-House archive review — quick guide


Hi {Name},

Thanks again for volunteering to help review the archive. Here's how it works.

I will share a Google Sheet with you. Please reply to this email and let me know the email address you prefer to use with Google Drive/email/docs etc.

The sheet has one tab per year from 1975 through 2026, skipping 2020 and 2021 (COVID years). Each row is one presentation. The white columns on the left show the title, authors, and abstract as automated optical character recognition (OCR) software read them out of the program PDFs. Where the OCR got something wrong, type the corrected version in the associated yellow column (title, authors, and/or abstract).

Each row also has a "View PDF" link in the source_pdf column that opens the original scanned program for that year in a new browser tab. You will want to have that open alongside the sheet as you work — OCR errors often only make sense when you can see the original page.


WHAT TO DO ON EACH ROW

1. Click "View PDF" in the source_pdf column to open the original program for that year. Use it as your reference throughout.

2. If the title, authors, or abstract is wrong, type the corrected version in the matching yellow column. Leave a yellow column blank if it was already right.

3. Set the "status" dropdown to one of:
     - fixed       you typed at least one correction
     - checked-ok  you read it and nothing needed changing
     - needs-help  something's wrong and you can't fix it from the sheet (e.g. an entry seems missing, or two abstracts got fused into one). Add a note in the "notes" column; I'll handle these.

4. Put your name in the "reviewer" column.


CONVENTIONS

- Author format: Lastname, Firstname and Lastname, Firstname. E.g.:
      LoLordo, Vincent M. and Jacobs, W. J. and Foree, Donald D.
  This handles compound surnames ("Van der Berg") and initial-only given names ("M.G. Yoon") without ambiguity.

- Leave the white columns alone. They're the read-only record of what the OCR captured.


TIME AND PACE

A careful pass through one year may take about 30-60 minutes depending on how rough the OCR was. There is no deadline. Do as much or as little as you want, in whichever years appeal to you.

Updates from the sheet are pulled into the main archive once a week, so your work will start showing up in the regenerated bibliography on that timeline.

Please let me know if you have any questions, or encounter troubles when working on this.

Thanks again,
Aaron
