Subject: In-House archive review — quick guide


Hi {Name},

Thanks again for volunteering to help review the archive. Here's how
it works.

I've shared a Google Sheet with you (let me know if you don't see it).
One tab per year from 1975 through 2026, skipping 2020 and 2021. Each
row is one presentation. The white columns on the left show the title,
authors, and abstract as automated optical character recognition (OCR)
software read them out of the program PDFs. Where the OCR got something
wrong, type the corrected version in the yellow column next to it.


WHAT TO DO ON EACH ROW

1. If the title, authors, or abstract is wrong, type the corrected
   version in the matching yellow column. Leave a yellow column blank
   if it was already right.

2. Set the "status" dropdown to one of:
     - fixed       you typed at least one correction
     - checked-ok  you read it and nothing needed changing
     - needs-help  something's wrong and you can't fix it from the sheet
                   (e.g. an entry seems missing, or two abstracts got
                   fused into one). Add a note in the "notes" column;
                   I'll handle these.

3. Put your name in the "reviewer" column.

4. The "source_pdf" column has a link straight to the original program
   PDF — click it to compare against the scan when in doubt.


CONVENTIONS

- Author format: Lastname, Firstname and Lastname, Firstname. E.g.:
      LoLordo, Vincent M. and Jacobs, W. J. and Foree, Donald D.
  This handles compound surnames ("Van der Berg") and initial-only
  given names ("M.G. Yoon") without ambiguity.

- Leave the white columns alone. They're the read-only record of what
  the OCR captured.


TIME AND PACE

Each year has 15-65 entries; a careful pass runs about 30-60 minutes
depending on how rough the OCR was. No deadline. Do as much or as
little as you want, in whichever years appeal to you. If you'd like
a suggestion of where to start, tell me what era you remember best
and I'll point you at the right tabs.

Updates from the sheet are pulled into the main archive once a week,
so your work will start showing up in the regenerated bibliography
soon after you make it.

Thanks again,
Aaron
